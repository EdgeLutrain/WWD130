The Why and The Main Page

The main page will be a display of the favorite pieces the Artist has done with the name of their website or the company they operate. A little bio about who they are and what type are they like. Have separate links to an FAQ, the gallery, and a contact page about commissions, the limitations, and the basic fees related to them. People will visit the site due to their interest in the Artist who would have this page set up. They often come from social media platforms to check out their work. It'll help them generate Commission leads as people look to understand their rates and what's possible from them. Expect questions like What type of art do they do? How much will you charge per the level of detail? Who is this Artist? And how do I get in contact with them to do commissions? We want them to be able to explore what the artists' previous works are. So that they have examples of understanding what they're capable of in the styles. Have some satisfied customer remarks about their art piece and the experience of getting a Commission. Have a frequently asked question form so they can resolve questions themselves if they are shy to reach out. Either a form to fill out to send to the Artist so they can reach out to the potential Commission or have details about how to contact him. Along with telling what they'll need to help get their Commission started as soon as possible. Ultimately, completing these actions requires going to the site. It acts as a FAQ form for the artists, and displaying it was possible. They were showing a Commission sheet of varying levels of detail and what they charge. It would be up to the Artist to not allow it to be subverted as they would save them a bunch of time answering repetitive questions and standardizing what is needed to begin the Commission process through the website.
